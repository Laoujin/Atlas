> **TL;DR for the talk.** AI didn't invent scams — it removed every last constraint on running them. In 2024 US victims lost a record $16.6B to internet crime [[1]](https://www.ic3.gov/AnnualReport/Reports/2024_IC3Report.pdf), Deloitte projects gen-AI fraud reaches $40B by 2027 [[8]](https://www.deloitte.com/us/en/insights/industry/financial-services/deepfake-banking-fraud-risk-on-the-rise.html), and Microsoft now measures AI-written phishing emails at a 54% click rate vs 12% for human-written [[54]](https://www.paubox.com/blog/microsoft-ai-makes-phishing-4.5x-more-effective-and-far-more-profitable). For a non-technical audience the shape worth conveying is: **the attacker side has industrialised** (Telegram-distributed jailbroken LLMs, real-time face-swap from one photo, $40B/year SE-Asia scam compounds), **the detector side is losing the arms race** (peer-reviewed accuracy drops 10-15% on unseen generators [[92]](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.06248)), and **the only consumer defenses that survive contact with reality are out-of-band human protocols** — family codewords, callback verification on a known number, and dual-officer sign-off on any video-call money instruction [[93]](https://www.ic3.gov/PSA/2025/PSA250515)[[95]](https://www.adaptivesecurity.com/blog/arup-deepfake-scam-attack).

## Three attacker playbooks worth showing on a slide

### 1. Voice cloning / vishing

Three seconds of TikTok audio is now enough to clone someone's voice with ~90% accuracy [[16]](https://www.unboxfuture.com/2026/04/ai-voice-cloning-scams-targeting.html). ElevenLabs officially recommends 1–2 minutes of clean audio for its Instant Voice Cloning, but acknowledges 30-second samples already produce excellent results [[15]](https://help.elevenlabs.io/hc/en-us/articles/13434364550801-How-many-voice-samples-should-I-upload-for-Instant-Voice-Cloning). OpenAI's Voice Engine (15-second clones) has stayed in restricted preview through 2026 specifically because of abuse risk [[21]](https://techcrunch.com/2025/03/06/a-year-later-openai-still-hasnt-released-its-voice-cloning-tool/)[[22]](https://openai.com/index/expanding-on-how-voice-engine-works-and-our-safety-research/). ElevenLabs added a "No-Go Voices" classifier that refuses clones of presidential candidates and other high-risk figures [[23]](https://help.elevenlabs.io/hc/en-us/articles/22584327690897-What-are-No-Go-Voices). The criminal market doesn't care: deepfake-vishing rose ~1,600% in Q1 2025 with turnkey vishing-as-a-service kits starting at $1,000 [[29]](https://dailysecurityreview.com/phishing/deepfake-vishing-incidents-surge-by-170-in-q2-2025/).

| Case | Date | What happened | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| LastPass | Apr 2024 | WhatsApp calls and a voicemail with a deepfake of CEO Karim Toubba's voice | Caught — wrong channel tipped off the employee [[17]](https://blog.lastpass.com/posts/attempted-audio-deepfake-call-targets-lastpass-employee)[[18]](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/lastpass-hackers-targeted-employee-in-failed-deepfake-ceo-call/) |
| NH Biden robocall | Jan 2024 | AI Biden voice telling NH Democrats not to vote in primary | $6M FCC fine to operative Steve Kramer; 13 felony voter-suppression counts; $1M penalty for distributor Lingo Telecom [[19]](https://www.fcc.gov/document/fcc-issues-6m-fine-nh-robocalls)[[20]](https://www.npr.org/2024/05/23/nx-s1-4977582/fcc-ai-deepfake-robocall-biden-new-hampshire-political-operative) |
| FBI senior-officials campaign | May 2025 | AI voice + SMS impersonating senior US officials | IC3 PSA; out-of-band callback the recommended counter [[93]](https://www.ic3.gov/PSA/2025/PSA250515) |
| AI scam losses (FBI 2025) | 2025 | Family-in-distress + investment deepfake calls | $893M total; $352M from victims 60+; AARP est. avg loss $18k, avg victim age 74 [[30]](https://www.aarp.org/money/scams-fraud/fbi-ftc-report-2025-losses/) |

Detection vendors push back hard but the consumer side is brutal: McAfee's 7,000-person survey found **70% of people are not confident they could distinguish a cloned voice from the real thing** [[26]](https://www.mcafee.com/content/dam/consumer/en-us/resources/cybersecurity/artificial-intelligence/rp-beware-the-artificial-impostor-report.pdf). Pindrop's enterprise Pulse product claims 99.2% accuracy from 2 seconds of audio and scored 96.4% (81/84) on NPR's benchmark [[24]](https://www.pindrop.com/product/pindrop-pulse/)[[25]](https://www.pindrop.com/article/npr-audio-deepfake-study/) — those numbers are for call-centers fronting banks, not your iPhone. NCC Group built a real-time clone-the-call rig in about an hour using public clips and off-the-shelf hardware [[31]](https://www.nccgroup.com/the-rising-threat-of-vishing-attacks-and-deepfakes/) — meaning the technical barrier is gone. Mandiant's M-Trends 2026 ranks voice phishing as the **#2 initial infection vector of 2025**, present in 11% of investigated intrusions [[32]](https://cyberscoop.com/social-engineering-surge-intrusion-vector-mandiant-m-trends/).

### 3. AI-generated phishing and the agentic frontier

The AI-phishing story has two tempos. The first, in 2023, was **cheaper-not-better**: IBM's X-Force study had ChatGPT generate a phishing email in 5 minutes vs ~16 hours human, but the AI version pulled an 11% click rate against humans' 14% [[50]](https://www.csoonline.com/article/656698/generative-ai-phishing-fears-realized-as-model-develops-highly-convincing-emails-in-5-minutes.html)[[51]](https://www.techrepublic.com/article/generative-ai-phishing-emails-impact/). Two years later that gap inverted.

| Study / report | Finding | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| Hoxhunt (Mar 2025), 70k simulations | AI agent "JKR" 24% more effective than elite human red teams; 55% relative improvement vs 2023 | AI **passes** human on persuasion [[52]](https://hoxhunt.com/blog/ai-powered-phishing-vs-humans) |
| Microsoft Digital Defense Report 2025 | AI-generated phish: **54% click rate vs 12% manual** (4.5×); up to 50× more profitable | The headline number for the talk [[54]](https://www.paubox.com/blog/microsoft-ai-makes-phishing-4.5x-more-effective-and-far-more-profitable)[[55]](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/security-insider/threat-landscape/microsoft-digital-defense-report-2025) |
| Microsoft MDDR 2025 | ClickFix social-engineering became #1 initial-access technique, 47% of Defender Expert cases | The volume side [[55]](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/security-insider/threat-landscape/microsoft-digital-defense-report-2025) |
| Hoxhunt 2026 Phishing Trends | AI-phish 4% (Nov 2025) → 56% (Dec 2025) of reported emails — 14× surge | The cliff edge [[53]](https://hoxhunt.com/guide/phishing-trends-report) |
| Verizon DBIR 2025 | Pretexting (BEC core) nearly doubled YoY; 30% of social-engineering | The BEC pipeline [[62]](https://abnormal.ai/blog/verizon-2025-dbir-key-takeaways) |
| FBI IC3 2024 | BEC: $2.77B / 21,442 incidents | The bottom line [[3]](https://abnormal.ai/blog/2024-fbi-ic3-report) |

The agentic frontier is now operational, not theoretical:

- **Anthropic's Nov 2025 disclosure** (a watershed for this talk): a Chinese state-sponsored group used Claude Code to autonomously target ~30 global organisations, with AI doing **80–90% of the campaign**, humans intervening at 4–6 critical decision points [[59]](https://www.anthropic.com/news/disrupting-AI-espionage).
- OpenAI banned North Korean Emerald Sleet accounts using ChatGPT to draft multilingual spear-phishing for APAC defense targets [[60]](https://openai.com/index/disrupting-malicious-uses-of-ai-by-state-affiliated-threat-actors/).
- Symantec demonstrated (Feb 2025) that OpenAI's Operator agent could be prompted to identify a person, find their email, write a PowerShell payload, and send a convincing lure end-to-end [[61]](https://www.darkreading.com/threat-intelligence/openai-operator-agent-proof-concept-phishing-attack).

## The supply chain: $40B/year of industrialised forced-labor scam compounds

This is the part most non-technical audiences don't know, and it changes the moral framing. The "Nigerian prince" mental model is two decades stale. The bulk of romance, pig-butchering, investment, and increasingly sextortion fraud now flows through industrial-scale compounds in Cambodia, Myanmar, and Laos.

- **Scale.** UNODC's April 2025 *Inflection Point* report estimates Mekong-region scam centres generate **~US$40B/year** in profits [[63]](https://www.unodc.org/unodc/frontpage/2025/April/cyberfraud-in-the-mekong-reaches-inflection-point--unodc-reveals.html). UN reporting documents torture, rape, and forced labor inside the centres, staffed by trafficked workers from 50+ countries [[64]](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/02/1167012). The 2024 US TIP Report kept Cambodia and Burma on Tier 3, citing tens of thousands of forced-criminality victims and senior-official complicity [[79]](https://www.state.gov/reports/2024-trafficking-in-persons-report/cambodia/).
- **Crypto rails.** Chainalysis tracked ~40% YoY pig-butchering revenue growth, a **210% jump in deposits** to those scams in 2024 [[12]](https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/news/romance-baiting-losses-surge-40/), and a **1,900% CAGR for AI service vendors** on the Huione marketplace 2021–2024 [[67]](https://www.chainalysis.com/blog/2024-pig-butchering-scam-revenue-grows-yoy/). Crypto-scam revenue hit $14–17B in 2025 [[69]](https://www.pymnts.com/fraud-attack/2026/chainalysis-says-ai-tools-helped-drive-crypto-scam-losses-to-14-billion-in-2025/). TRM Labs testified to Congress that FinCEN-designated Cambodian Huione Group received **$39.6B** in transaction volume; Chinese-language laundering networks processed $103B in 2025, up from $123M in 2020 [[14]](https://www.congress.gov/119/meeting/house/119126/witnesses/HHRG-119-HM11-Wstate-RedbordA-20260421.pdf).
- **The Wang Xing inflection point.** Chinese actor Wang Xing was lured to Mae Sot on a fake film audition on 3 Jan 2025, kidnapped into Myawaddy, and rescued on 7 Jan [[70]](https://www.humanrightsresearch.org/post/wang-xing-s-nightmare-how-myawaddy-became-a-hub-of-fraud-and-human-trafficking). The case sparked a Chinese-tourism boycott; on 5 Feb 2025 Thailand cut electricity, internet, and fuel to Myawaddy compounds, and 12–17 Feb Myanmar repatriated 261 victims and detained 1,303 foreign nationals [[71]](https://www.usip.org/publications/2025/02/china-exploits-thailands-crackdown-scam-compounds-grow-security-influence).
- **Enforcement.** INTERPOL's 2024 anti-trafficking-fuelled-fraud operation across 116 countries arrested 2,500+ [[65]](https://www.interpol.int/en/News-and-Events/News/2025/INTERPOL-releases-new-information-on-globalization-of-scam-centres); Operation Shadow Storm (2026) cited $11B in compound-linked crypto flows [[66]](https://www.amlintelligence.com/2026/03/news-interpol-launches-operation-shadow-storm-to-fight-scam-centres/). OFAC sanctioned the Karen National Army and leader Saw Chit Thu (May 2025) [[73]](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0129) and the DKBA + Trans Asia/Troth Star network behind KK Park and Huanya (Nov 2025), citing **$10B in 2024 American losses, +66% YoY** [[72]](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0312). October 2025: US prosecutors seized **$15B in crypto** from Chen Zhi's Cambodian forced-labor camps — dwarfing the prior $225M record [[81]](https://www.cnn.com/2025/10/14/politics/cryptocurrency-investment-scheme-charges-forced-labor-camps).
- **AI amplification.** Real-time face-swap apps like Haotian (50 tunable facial settings) drove a 700% Q1-2025 spike in US deepfake fraud and >$200M in Q1 losses [[77]](https://contentauthenticity.org/blog/march-2025-this-month-in-generative-ai-ai-powered-romance-scams)[[78]](https://itmagazine.com/2025/12/19/behind-the-scenes-of-the-ultra-realistic-ai-face-swapping-platform-fueling-romance-scams/) — these are the tools used inside the compounds during video calls.
- **Sextortion + nudify-app combo.** NCMEC's 2025 CyberTipline received **21.3M reports**; online-enticement rose 156% YoY to 1.4M; **>1.5M reports linked to generative AI** [[74]](https://www.missingkids.org/blog/2026/the-work-never-stops-first-look-at-ncmecs-2025-data). Since 2021 NCMEC has tracked **at least 36 teenage boys** who died by suicide after sextortion [[75]](https://www.missingkids.org/blog/2025/ncmec-on-the-hill-online-child-exploitation-escalating-more-violent). The Jordan DeMay case — a Marquette teenager who killed himself within hours of being extorted for $1,000 he didn't have — yielded 17.5-year sentences for two Nigerian brothers, Samuel and Sampson Ogoshi [[76]](https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/two-nigerians-sentenced-years-sextortion-case-led-michigan-teens-rcna169730). The FBI's earlier 322% sextortion spike (Feb 2022 – Feb 2023) was already alarming; AI "nudify" apps are the now-dominant 2024–26 escalation, and victims are overwhelmingly boys aged 14–17 [[80]](https://www.foxnews.com/media/fbi-warns-ai-deepfakes-being-create-sextortion-schemes).

For the talk: the audience needs to know **the average voice on the other end of a romance scam is itself a victim** — UNODC documents trafficked workers from 50+ countries forced to run the calls under threat of violence [[63]](https://www.unodc.org/unodc/frontpage/2025/April/cyberfraud-in-the-mekong-reaches-inflection-point--unodc-reveals.html)[[64]](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/02/1167012). That reframes the problem from "stupid grandma" to organised transnational forced-labor crime.

## What actually works in 2026 — four layers, none sufficient alone

| Layer | What it is | Where it bites | Where it fails |

Platform-side, **labelling exists but is voluntary** and patchy. TikTok auto-labels via C2PA + watermark + detection and has flagged 1.3B AI videos, though AI-written captions/hashtags are exempt [[98]](https://storrito.com/resources/tiktoks-2026-ai-labeling-rules-and-what-they-signal-for-platform-governance/). Meta replaced its "Made with AI" label with "AI info" in July 2024, driven by self-declaration and partner metadata [[99]](https://transparency.meta.com/governance/tracking-impact/labeling-ai-content/). And the defender's own AI is now a phishing surface: in 2025 a prompt-injection flaw in Gmail's Gemini "summarize this email" feature smuggled invisible white-on-white instructions through, producing fake Google security alerts inside the AI summary itself [[96]](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/google-gemini-flaw-hijacks-email-summaries-for-phishing/).

The "**liar's dividend**" — bad actors dismissing real footage as deepfake — is now a recognised democratic harm. The Brennan Center, citing Hany Farid, argues that without ubiquitous provenance infrastructure the dividend grows; rapid authentication is the practical counter [[97]](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/research-reports/deepfakes-elections-and-shrinking-liars-dividend).

## Concrete advice the talk can leave on the screen

For a non-technical room, three rules survive contact with reality:

1. **Family codeword.** Pick one with your parents, partner, and kids. If a "distressed family member" calls and can't say it, hang up and call the known number. The data is unambiguous: FBI logged $893M in AI-scam losses in 2025 ($352M from victims 60+); AARP puts the average voice-clone victim at 74 with ~$18,000 lost [[30]](https://www.aarp.org/money/scams-fraud/fbi-ftc-report-2025-losses/); McAfee finds 70% of people can't tell a clone from the real voice [[26]](https://www.mcafee.com/content/dam/consumer/en-us/resources/cybersecurity/artificial-intelligence/rp-beware-the-artificial-impostor-report.pdf).
2. **Out-of-band callback for any money instruction received via video, voice, or chat.** This is the one rule the LastPass employee followed [[18]](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/lastpass-hackers-targeted-employee-in-failed-deepfake-ceo-call/), the Arup employee did not [[27]](https://www.cnn.com/2024/02/04/asia/deepfake-cfo-scam-hong-kong-intl-hnk), and the IC3 explicitly recommends [[93]](https://www.ic3.gov/PSA/2025/PSA250515). For organisations: dual-officer counter-signature on every transfer, always [[95]](https://www.adaptivesecurity.com/blog/arup-deepfake-scam-attack).
3. **Assume the romance is a forced-labor compound, not a person.** UN: ~$40B/year industry, 50+ countries' citizens trafficked in [[63]](https://www.unodc.org/unodc/frontpage/2025/April/cyberfraud-in-the-mekong-reaches-inflection-point--unodc-reveals.html)[[64]](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/02/1167012). Crypto requests = stop. Reverse-image search the profile photo. Insist on a live, unscheduled video call — and even then, knowing Haotian-class face-swap exists [[78]](https://itmagazine.com/2025/12/19/behind-the-scenes-of-the-ultra-realistic-ai-face-swapping-platform-fueling-romance-scams/), treat that as one signal, not proof.

The bigger frame for the audience: **defenses now layer rather than solve.** Provenance + regulation + detection + human protocols, each imperfect, get you to "good enough" — provided you're disciplined enough to use them. The AI security story in 2026 is less "the machines outsmarted us" and more "the economic floor under fraud collapsed, and only people who slow down get to keep their money." The talk should land that as the takeaway.
