TL;DR — top picks for a Belgian senior dev shipping applied ML in 2026
  1. Pick one local low-friction event: Devoxx Belgium (Oct 5-9, Antwerp) for the broadest Java/AI/cloud agenda within 1h of Brussels[20].
  2. Pick one applied-ML deep-dive: ML Prague (May 4-6, €590 +VAT for conf+workshops) or Applied AI Conference Berlin (May 28, one-day) — both target engineers shipping LLMs/agents to prod[5][9].
  3. Skip the festival giants (Web Summit, WAICF, AI Summit London) unless you're sourcing partners or hiring — exhibit-floor heavy, signal-to-noise low for technical work[15].
  4. If you can only attend one in autumn and want both research depth and applied content: PyData Amsterdam (Sep 10-11) for production data-science talks; ECML PKDD (Sep 7-11, Naples) if you'll read the proceedings anyway[8][3].

Today is May 1, 2026, so this calendar splits into still upcoming (the cards below) and already past (collapsed list at the bottom — useful for citing video archives or planning 2027). Each card carries the official URL, date, venue, target audience, and ticket band where published. Categories are fuzzy by design: a "festival" card may be more useful than an "academic" card depending on your goal (hiring vs. publishing vs. learning).

Applied / practitioner conferences

These are the events with the highest density of "we shipped X to production and here's what broke" talks. Best signal-per-day ratio for a senior dev.

Machine Learning Prague

May 4-6, 2026Prague (O2 Universum)

11th edition. Hybrid. ~1,000 attendees, 45 international speakers from Meta, NVIDIA, AWS, Barclays. Tracks span LLMs/genAI, recsys/search, computer vision, financial ML, enterprise agents, edge AI[5].

Late Bird: conf €350 / workshops €290 / combined €590 (+VAT)[5]

Applied AI Conference Berlin

May 28, 2026Berlin (The Delta Campus)

One day, two stages, explicitly for "teams building & running AI systems in production." Speakers from Microsoft, Google DeepMind, Stripe, OpenAI on agents, LLM optimisation, safety evals, deployment[9].

Pricing on request (gated)[9]

PyData Amsterdam

Sep 10-11, 2026Amsterdam (NDSM Loods)

The strongest PyData of the year by speaker draw — Hannes Mühleisen (DuckDB Labs), Judith Dijk (European Defence Agency), Demetrios Brinkmann (MLOps Community). Two days, lots of production-data-science talks[8].

Tickets via Pretix (super-early-bird at time of writing)[8]

PyData London

Jun 5-7, 2026London (Convene Sancroft, St Paul's)

Three days, audience explicitly "data scientists, data engineers, and developers of data analysis tools." Keynotes pull from Pydantic and AI research engineering[7].

Tickets via ti.to (pricing via nav)[7]

Data Innovation Summit Stockholm

May 6-8, 2026Stockholm (Kistamässan)

11th edition, 3,500+ attendees, 15 stages. Day 3 is a new vertical-industry day (Finance, Manufacturing, Retail, Healthcare, Telecom, Logistics, Public Sector, Gaming). Hackathon DIS2026X1 deploys an AI agent onto a physical robot[11].

Rise of AI Conference

May 5-6, 2026Berlin

10th-anniversary edition. Programmes split between policy/funding (founders, investors, policymakers) and an Applied AI Stage on launching, scaling, hiring, going global[10].

ai-PULSE

Oct 13, 2026Paris (Station F)

One-day technical conference at Station F. Keynote line-up includes Yann LeCun (Meta) plus speakers from Salesforce, NVIDIA, and AI startups. Smaller and denser than the festival circuit[25].

Academic flagships in Europe

If you publish or read papers, these are the European-hosted peer-reviewed venues running in 2026. Workshops are usually the most "applied" days — worth attending even without a paper.

IJCAI-ECAI 2026

Aug 15-21, 2026Bremen, Germany

Joint year for the International Joint Conference on AI and the European Conference on AI. Main track plus five special tracks: Human-Centered AI, AI for Social Good, AI4Tech, AI and Health, AI and Robotics[1].

ECML PKDD 2026

Sep 7-11, 2026Naples (Univ. Federico II)

The European ML + Knowledge Discovery flagship. Main building at Via Claudio 21; plenaries 500m away at Mostra d'Oltremare. Five-day format with workshops bracketing the main programme[3].

ECCV 2026

Sep 8-12, 2026Malmö, Sweden

Biennial European Conference on Computer Vision (no ECCV in odd years; no ICCV in 2026). Workshops/tutorials Sep 8-9, main + expo Sep 10-12 at Malmö Arena and Malmömässan[2]. ⚠ Overlaps ECML PKDD by two days — pick one.

Big-tent / festival events

Industrial scale, exhibit-heavy, multi-track. Usually a paid keynote/networking play; rarely the place to learn the implementation details of anything. Useful for sourcing vendors, hiring, or introducing non-technical stakeholders.

The AI Summit London

Jun 10-11, 2026London (Tobacco Dock)

10th edition, embedded in London Tech Week. 5,000+ attendees, 300+ speakers, 10 stages, 100+ exhibitors. Speaker bench includes UK Government, IBM, Barclays, JPMorgan, M&S, Virgin Atlantic[13].

Passes £25-£1,399; workshop "Mastering AI: From Prompts to Agents" £599/day, cap 150[14]

RAISE Summit

Jul 8-9, 2026Paris (Carrousel du Louvre)

9,000+ attendees including Fortune 500, investors, founders. Tagline-heavy ("ROI-driven AI, agentic systems, sovereign AI" — the "4Fs framework"). Higher exec/investor density than tech[15].

€699-€4,599 (+VAT)[15]

World Summit AI

Oct 7-8, 2026Zaandam (TAETS Art & Event Park)

10th anniversary; ~120 speakers; sits inside the broader World AI Week (Oct 5-9, 2026, ~15,000 attendees). Strong on AI ethics, regulation, reasoning systems[16].

Web Summit Lisbon

Nov 9-12, 2026Lisbon

The continent's biggest tech-festival headcount: 30,000+ founders, investors, leaders. AI Summit track theme this year: "Designing intelligence, disrupting society" — generative AI, model deployment, infra, regulation, enterprise adoption[17][21].

Big Data Conference Europe

Nov 24-27, 2026Vilnius, Lithuania

Smaller (~700 attendees, 50% senior-level) but technically substantive — data science, ML, quantum computing. Easier to actually meet speakers vs. festival giants[23].

€700-€1,730 (+VAT)[23]

Developer / cloud-native conferences with strong AI tracks

Not AI-first, but where Belgian and adjacent dev communities cluster. Often the best local bet in terms of cost, language, and travel.

Devoxx Belgium

Oct 5-9, 2026Antwerp (Kinepolis)

Five days, ~3,200 devs. Java is the historical core but the AI/big-data track has grown substantially: agents, retrieval, LLM apps in JVM ecosystems. CFP runs Jun 1 - Jul 17, 2026[20]. Best home-turf option for a Belgian dev.

NVIDIA GTC Paris

June 2026 (TBC)Paris

Returns after the 2025 inaugural Paris edition (Dôme de Paris, Jun 11-12). Cloud AI infra, agentic AI, physical AI; aimed at CXOs, devs, researchers, startups[26]. ⚠ Confirm 2026 dates on the official NVIDIA GTC events page before booking.

World AI Cannes Festival (WAICF)

Feb 12-13, 2026 (past)Cannes

Already past, listed for reference / 2027 planning. 10,000+ visitors, four tracks (AI in Business, Governance, Next-Gen Tech, Health AI)[12][22].

Scope notes

  • "Europe" here means the geographic continent (incl. UK, Switzerland, Lithuania); excludes virtual-only events and non-European conferences with European satellites.
  • "AI / applied-ML" is interpreted broadly: anything where the core programme spends >~30% on ML/AI content. Pure data-engineering conferences (Snowflake/Databricks roadshows) are excluded; Devoxx and KubeCon make the list because their 2026 editions had explicit AI tracks taking up significant programme share.
  • Ticket prices are taken from official pricing pages where available (May 2026); some festival events publish only on request — flagged in each card.
  • NeurIPS and ICLR are not in Europe in 2026. ICCV is biennial (odd years only). The European-hosted research flagships in 2026 are ECCV, ECML PKDD, IJCAI-ECAI.
