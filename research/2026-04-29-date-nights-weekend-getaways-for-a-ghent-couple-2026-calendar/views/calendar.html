The verdict — three weekends do most of the work

1–17 May clusters Floraliën, the Bruges Holy Blood procession and Gent Smaakt into a single fortnight where you barely leave home [1][2][3]. 17–26 July is Gentse Feesten on your doorstep [4]. 17–22 November is the Six Days at 't Kuipke — a 166 m banked velodrome wrapped in a beer-tent atmosphere, ten minutes from home [5]. Build the year around those three; treat everything else as optional.

PILLAR 01 · MAY

1–17 May

The Ghent fortnight

Floraliën runs ten days, the Holy Blood procession is the Ascension Thursday in the middle of it, and Gent Smaakt closes the long weekend on Korenmarkt. You barely have to leave home and you still hit two flagship events.[1][2][3]

The strongest in-city add-ons: OPUS at Capitole on May 7/9/10[16], Sol Gabetta at De Bijloke on May 9[17].

PILLAR 02 · JULY

17–26 July

Gentse Feesten on your doorstep

Ten free days, ~2 million visitors, every square in central Ghent live with music, theatre, food.[4][27] Polé Polé runs the waterways in parallel.

Tomorrowland heads-up: 17–19 + 24–26 July passes are sold out — expect doubled regional fares and crowds.[8]

PILLAR 03 · NOVEMBER

17–22 Nov

Six Days of Ghent at 't Kuipke

166 m banked indoor velodrome, ten minutes from home, riders flying past at eye level — and a side of the venue runs as a Flemish café with live MC, beer, and music between heats.[5][28]

Doors: 18:00 → midnight on weeknights. Book: Friday/Saturday sessions sell first.

The single-Tuesday UNESCO fork
Sun 15 → Tue 17 February 2026

Aalst

35 min by train · UNESCO 2010
  • Satirical floats, the closing-night Voil Jeanetten (men in drag flooding the Grote Markt)[15]
  • A puppet ceremonially burned to end Lent's eve
  • Pick this if: you want raucous + close. Better as a half-day return.
or

Binche

~2 h via Brussels · UNESCO Masterpiece 2003
  • The Gilles in wax masks & ostrich-feather hats throw blood oranges into the crowd on Shrove Tuesday[14]
  • Older and stranger than Aalst
  • Pick this if: you want heritage. Won't repeat anywhere else in Europe.

The booking rule that pays for itself

Always book Eurostar/TGV/ICE from Gent-Sint-Pieters

Since 15 December 2024 international tickets are valid only at the named Belgian station — but if you book the through fare from Gent-Sint-Pieters rather than Brussels-Midi, the SNCB feeder leg keeps same-day flexibility.[11] Lille is the only direct international from Ghent (~1h08)[12]; everything else routes through Brussels. Book Paris and London four-plus months out for the cheap fares.[13]

The Christmas-market trade-off

Three world-class markets overlap. Pick one.

Don't try all three — pick the one that is the trip and let Ghent's market be the walk-by from home.

Ghent
3 Dec – 3 Jan · walk-by from home

150+ chalets, 44 m Ferris wheel, ice rink, "Winter Wonder Castle" at Gravensteen.[23]

Cologne Cathedral
25 Nov – 23 Dec · ~3 h via Brussels

150+ stalls under the cathedral spires, 3 min from the central station.[24]

Strasbourg ★
27 Nov – 27 Dec · ~4h15 via Brussels

~300 chalets across the Grande-Île; one of two daily direct TGVs from Brussels.[25][26] If you make it the weekend trip, pick this.
