TL;DR. For a quick fix from Ghent: bike or paddle the Bourgoyen-Ossemeersen marshes after work[2]. For a weekend, head to the Belgian Ardennes — kayak the Lesse from Houyet (~€20 pp, 3 hrs)[9] and sleep in a Durbuy treehouse with an outdoor jacuzzi (~€140/night)[26]. For one big memorable splurge: a sunrise hot-air balloon over Bruges (€160 pp, champagne on landing)[13]. Anchor the year around three set-pieces — tulip cycling near Keukenhof in late April[29], golden beech walking in the Sonian Forest mid-October[35], and stargazing the Milky Way from Signal de Botrange in February[17].

Practical notes

  • Book the balloon first. Bruges Ballooning operates Apr–Oct only, sells out on weekends, and weather can scrub flights — pick a 2-night stay so you have a buffer day[11].
  • Lesse kayaking has water-level requirements (Apr–Oct only)[8]. Houyet is a direct train from Ghent via Brussels–Namur — the kayak company runs a return shuttle, so no car needed.
  • Marche-les-Dames is weekends-only. The site is wedged into an active military training zone; midweek access is denied[25].
  • Cycling Through Water and Cycling Through the Trees can be combined. Both sit in Limburg's cycle-node network[41] — about 35 km between them; rent at Bokrijk, drop in Hechtel.
  • For dark skies near home, Signal de Botrange and the Eupen-Malmedy plateau are the only Belgian spots where the Milky Way is naked-eye visible[17]. Best on a moonless winter night when the bog mist has cleared.
  • Cycle-route tip. Visit Flanders publishes free GPX downloads for the entire numbered cycle-node network[41]; pair with the AllTrails Ghent set for foot-only trails[39].
