**Three Ghent weekends do most of the work.** 1–17 May clusters [Floraliën](https://floralien.be/), the [Bruges Holy Blood procession](https://www.holyblood.com/), and [Gent Smaakt](https://www.gentsmaakt.be/) into a single fortnight where you barely leave home [[1]](https://floralien.be/en/welcome/)[[2]](https://www.visitbruges.be/en/whats-on/events-calendar/procession-holy-blood-2026)[[3]](https://www.gentsmaakt.be/en/practical-info/); 17–26 July is [Gentse Feesten](https://gentsefeesten.stad.gent/en) on your doorstep [[4]](https://visit.gent.be/en/calendar/ghent-festivities); 17–22 November is the [Six Days](https://www.lottozesdaagse.be/) at [&apos;t Kuipke](https://stadiongent.be/) — a 166 m banked velodrome wrapped in a beer-tent atmosphere, ten minutes from home [[5]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Six_Days_of_Ghent). Build the year around those three and treat everything else as optional.

**What's *not* happening in 2026.** [Bruges Triennial](https://triennalebrugge.be/) does not run [[6]](https://triennalebrugge.be/en/calendar/triennale-brugge-spaces-of-possibility); [Ghent's Light Festival](https://lichtfestival.stad.gent/) is triennial and skips this year — next edition 2027 [[7]](https://visit.gent.be/en/calendar/ghent-light-festival); [Tomorrowland](https://www.tomorrowland.com/) passes are sold out [[8]](https://www.jambase.com/festival/tomorrowland-belgium-2026); [Les Thermes de Spa](https://thermesdespa.com/) closes 7–12 June for maintenance, so book around it [[9]](https://thermesdespa.com/prices-and-opening-hours/). The compensation prize: Brussels's [Flower Carpet](https://www.flowercarpet.brussels/) returns to the Grand-Place 13–16 August with a Japan theme — biennial, so 2026 is the year [[10]](https://www.brussels.be/flower-carpet).

**The booking rule that pays for itself.** Since 15 December 2024 international tickets are valid only at the named Belgian station — but if you book Eurostar/TGV/ICE *from "Gent-Sint-Pieters"* rather than Brussels-Midi, the through fare keeps your SNCB feeder leg with same-day flexibility [[11]](https://www.b-europe.com/EN/Questions/Tickets). Lille is the only direct international from Ghent (~1h08) [[12]](https://www.thetrainline.com/en/train-times/ghent-to-lille-flandres); everything else routes through Brussels. Book Paris and London four-plus months out for the cheap fares [[13]](https://wise.com/gb/blog/eurostar-destinations-from-london).

**The single-Tuesday UNESCO call.** Aalst and Binche carnivals fall on the same 15–17 February. Binche is the canonical Masterpiece — the Gilles throw blood oranges into the crowd on Shrove Tuesday [[14]](https://ich.unesco.org/en/RL/carnival-of-binche-00033); Aalst is 35 min by train and more raucous [[15]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carnival_of_Aalst). You cannot do both. Decide once between heritage and proximity.

**Ghent itself is the best venue.** The strongest in-city picks are also the most atmospheric: Christos Papadopoulos's *OPUS* Bach ballet at [Capitole Gent](https://capitolegent.be/) on May 7, 9, 10 [[16]](https://bachtrack.com/dance-event/opus-opera-ballet-vlaanderen-opera-vlaanderen-ghent-7-may-2026/425468); Sol Gabetta with the [Antwerp Symphony](https://www.antwerpsymphonyorchestra.be/) at [De Bijloke](https://www.bijloke.be/) (May 9) in a 13th-century hospital hall [[17]](https://www.bijloke.be/nl/programma/Klassiek); [Gent Jazz](https://www.gentjazz.com/) on the same Bijlokesite, July 2–18 — walk home [[18]](https://www.gentjazz.com/en); a private [MIGLOT](https://miglot.com/) perfume workshop capped at two (€545, 2.5 h — the format *is* the date) [[19]](https://miglot.com/en/products/miglot-perfume-workshop-private). For escape, the Ardennes shortlist is unusually deep: kayak the Lesse from Houyet (~€20 pp) [[20]](https://www.kayaksemois-ardenne.be/eng/kayak-belgique), sleep in [Treelodge Retie](https://www.treelodge.be/) or a Durbuy treehouse with a private hot tub [[21]](https://www.treelodge.be/en/), pilgrimage once a year to [Les Thermes de Spa](https://thermesdespa.com/) [[22]](https://thermesdespa.com/prices-and-opening-hours/).

**The Christmas-market trade-off.** Three world-class markets overlap: Ghent 3 Dec – 3 Jan [[23]](https://visit.gent.be/en/calendar/christmas-market), [Cologne Cathedral](https://www.koelnerweihnachtsmarkt.com/) 25 Nov – 23 Dec [[24]](https://www.colognecathedral.de/christmas-market.html), [Strasbourg](https://noel.strasbourg.eu/) 27 Nov – 27 Dec [[25]](https://tourismattractions.net/france/strasbourg-christmas-markets-2026-schedule). Don't try all three — pick the one that *is* the trip (Strasbourg, ~4h15 with one of two daily direct TGVs from Brussels [[26]](https://www.omio.com/trains/brussels/strasbourg)) and let Ghent's market be the walk-by from home.
