For the splurge afternoon

MIGLOT private perfume — €545 / 2.5h

Capped at two participants. The format is the date. Walk out with a 50ml Eau de Parfum in a leather travel-spray case, plus two refills.[4]

For the cheap memorable hour

Gruut city brewery — €15/pers / 50min

Three-beer guided tasting in a central canal-side brewery. Min two people, must reserve in advance. The lowest cost-per-memory on this list.[7]

For the best half-day combo

Onderbergen 4A — chocolate + beer / 2h

Praline-making from scratch with an expert chocolatier, paired with a Belgian-beer tasting in the same sitting. Free cancel up to 24h.[3]

Heads-upWhat not to plan around in 2026

  • GlazenHuis Lommel — Flanders' centre for contemporary glass art and the obvious Belgian glassblowing venue — is closed for renovations until spring 2027. Use the Brussels Ambacht in Beeld masterclass on 9–10 May instead.[24]
  • The Van Gogh Brussels Immersive Experience has a closing window in March — confirm dates on Fever before booking. It has been winding down. Visit Gent and Visit Brussels official channels are the canonical sources for live dates.
  • Visit Gent confirms MIGLOT is the city's first Belgian perfume house — useful framing when introducing the workshop to a partner.[6]

tactics

Notes from the editor

Couples-only formats
MIGLOT private perfume[4] and Suzy's home cooking class[10] are the only Ghent options where the format is built for a pair rather than slotted into a group of strangers. Everything else is "you'll share a table".
Two days ahead is the realistic minimum
For Gruut, MIGLOT and any chocolate workshop on a weekend slot. Saturdays are the first to sell out — book a weekday evening if you have flexibility.[7][1]
MIGLOT Saturday slots fill first
The atelier explicitly notes Saturdays through the season are first to fill.[4]
Treat Bokrijk as a full Saturday
Combine three of the six €5 workshops into one day rather than booking a single one — the day rate makes the trip out to Genk worth the train ride.[18]
