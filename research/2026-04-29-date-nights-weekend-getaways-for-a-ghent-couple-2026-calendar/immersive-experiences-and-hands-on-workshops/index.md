## Weekend-getaway picks (workshops as the centre of gravity)

### Bokrijk (Genk, Limburg) — open-air craft museum

Six rotating €5 hands-on workshops on a visit day: throw a clay pot at a turntable, knead and bake your own bread roll, stamp a leather bracelet, screen-print a tote, etc.[[18]](https://bokrijk.be/en/workshops). Watch traditional baking, blacksmithing, weaving and shoe-making demos in between. Pair with a one-night stay in Genk or nearby Hasselt and a visit to the Jenever Museum the next morning.

### Bruges — chocolate + beer + waffles

Same operator as Ghent, so you get the **same praline/truffle workshop format** (€56 / €42) at Grauwwerkersstraat 15[[19]](https://www.belgianchocolateworkshop.com/). Add the **Bruges Beer Experience** museum + 3-beer tasting[[20]](https://www.mybeerexperience.com/en) and the **Waffles 'n Beer Workshop** (bake-your-own waffles + 3 Belgian beers)[[21]](https://www.viator.com/tours/Bruges/The-Waffles-n-Beer-Workshop/d4836-121907P1). One overnight covers all three comfortably.

### Brussels — for the immersive-art weekend

ARTCADE[[13]](https://enterartcade.be/en/) + Ambacht in Beeld glassblowing masterclass[[17]](https://ambachtinbeeldfestival.be/en/soort/workshops-masterclasses/) (only 9–10 May 2026) is a very strong combo if you can hit that weekend.

## Heads-up — what NOT to plan around

- **GlazenHuis Lommel** (Flanders' centre for contemporary glass art and the obvious Belgian glass-blowing workshop venue) → **closed for renovations until spring 2027**[[24]](https://glazenhuis.be/en/). Use the Brussels Ambacht in Beeld masterclass on 9–10 May instead.
- The **Van Gogh Brussels Immersive Experience** has a closing window in March; confirm dates on Fever before booking — it has been winding down. (Visit Gent + Visit Brussels official channels are the canonical source for live dates if in doubt.)
- **Visit.gent.be MIGLOT** confirms the Ghent fragrance lab is the city's first Belgian perfume house[[6]](https://visit.gent.be/en/see-do/miglot-fragrance-lab) — useful framing when introducing the workshop to your partner.

## Booking tactics

- **Couples-only formats:** MIGLOT private perfume[[4]](https://miglot.com/en/products/miglot-perfume-workshop-private) and Suzy's home cooking class[[10]](https://www.travelingspoon.com/hosts/4461-authentic-belgian-cooking-class-in-a-beautiful-home-in-ghent) are the only Ghent options where the format is built for a pair rather than slotted into a group of strangers. Everything else is "you'll share a table".
- **2 days ahead is the realistic minimum** for Gruut, MIGLOT and any chocolate workshop on a weekend slot[[7]](https://www.gruut.be/en/visit-and-reservation/)[[1]](https://www.ghentchocolateworkshop.com/).
- **Saturdays book out fastest** — MIGLOT explicitly notes Saturday slots through the season are the first to fill[[4]](https://miglot.com/en/products/miglot-perfume-workshop-private). Book a weekday evening if you have flexibility.
- For Bokrijk, treat it as a full Saturday — combine three of the six €5 workshops into one day rather than booking a single one[[18]](https://bokrijk.be/en/workshops).
