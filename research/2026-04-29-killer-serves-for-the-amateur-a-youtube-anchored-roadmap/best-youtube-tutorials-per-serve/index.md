## Serve 1 — short pendulum (backspin / no-spin pair)

The pair is the highest-leverage serve at club level — same arm path, two completely different balls. Two videos cover the package.

| # | Video | Channel | Length / views | Why this one |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **[4 killer serves to destroy your opponents](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EuXKHxRcRbQ)** [[26]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EuXKHxRcRbQ) | Tom Lodziak × Craig Bryant | 11:37, **9.2M views** [[26]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EuXKHxRcRbQ) | The most-viewed serve tutorial on YouTube; pendulum is one of the four. | 2 | **[Backspin and No Spin Serve Variations](https://www.pingskills.com/tutorials/table-tennis-serving-strategy/back-spin-and-no-spin-serve-variations)** [[13]](https://www.pingskills.com/tutorials/table-tennis-serving-strategy/back-spin-and-no-spin-serve-variations) | PingSkills | Tutorial page | The dedicated pair-tutorial; explicit 3rd-ball-attack framing |
| 3 | [Sidespin (pendulum) serve — tips and tactics](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NFCgf-41SmM) [[6]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NFCgf-41SmM) | Tom Lodziak | ~502K views, 2017 [[6]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NFCgf-41SmM) | Lodziak's own deepest pendulum walk-through, pre-Bryant collab |
| 4 | [Short Pendulum Serve Tutorial \| Backspin](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=il64P9iwDfg) [[1]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=il64P9iwDfg) | MLFM Table Tennis | 26.6K views, Apr 2020 [[1]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=il64P9iwDfg) | The most directly on-topic title for the *short* pendulum specifically |
| 5 | [FOOL Your Opponents: Change the Spin of Your Pendulum Serves](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ol3WDm4g1Wk) [[19]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ol3WDm4g1Wk) | TableTennisDaily | 4 min, May 2025 [[19]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ol3WDm4g1Wk) | Most current single-motion spin-switch demo |

The load-bearing cue from all of them: **contact near the handle** for no-spin (the blade has moved through a smaller arc, so it imparts less spin) [[14]](https://www.pingskills.com/table-tennis-forum/tips-for-a-basic-no-spin-serve), keep the arm path identical, brush under the ball for backspin, straighten the bat angle at the last moment for no-spin [[5]](https://www.tabletenniscoach.me.uk/question/serve-variation/) [[11]](https://www.tabletenniscoach.me.uk/the-surprising-power-of-a-no-spin-serve/). EmRatThich frames the principle directly: *"change the contact point but keep the same serve motion"* [[15]](https://pingsunday.com/what-is-a-good-serve-in-table-tennis/). Two community supplements that earn their keep: **tap flat instead of brushing** on the no-spin twin, and **stomp the foot at contact** to mask the softer ball-blade sound [[17]](https://www.tabletennisdaily.com/forum/topics/float-serving.9470/).

**Drill scaffolding before you watch:** Larry Hodges' off-table-then-on-table progression — toss-and-spin off the table first, then contact near the very bottom of the ball ~6 inches up, first bounce near the net so the second bounce dies on opponent's end-line [[9]](https://blog.paddlepalace.com/2013/08/a-step-by-step-sequence-to-learning-pendulum-serves/). EmRatThich adds: loose grip, bat tilted slightly inward, start slow-and-spinny before adding speed, with a legal ~16 cm vertical toss [[10]](https://pingsunday.com/forehand-pendulum-serve-tutorial/).

**Grip prerequisite:** PingSkills' "first step" — drop the three lower fingers off the handle into a fist, grip with thumb and index only, slide the index further onto the backhand rubber [[4]](https://www.pingskills.com/blog/the-first-step-to-a-great-pendulum-serve). Without this grip change the wrist can't snap fast enough to get heavy spin.

| 4 | [FAST Pendulum Serve Tutorial \| No Spin](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rEkkXXz-1ZQ) [[22]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rEkkXXz-1ZQ) | MLFM Table Tennis | ~2 min, May 2020 | Specifically the *fast no-spin* variant — mimic the spin-serve motion at higher pace [[22]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rEkkXXz-1ZQ) |

The technique converges across all four: low toss, **contact at or below net height** (let the ball drop further than feels comfortable), aim the first bounce on your *own* endline corner so the trajectory stays flat and forward [[27]](https://www.tabletenniscoach.me.uk/destroy-opponents-long-serves/) [[29]](https://www.experttabletennis.com/how-to-serve-faster-in-table-tennis/). Targets: elbow / crossover, or wide deep backhand [[27]](https://www.tabletenniscoach.me.uk/destroy-opponents-long-serves/).

**Frequency rule:** 2–3 per game vs attackers, but Lodziak says you can ride it up to ~80% vs passive opponents who don't punish you [[28]](https://www.tabletenniscoach.me.uk/how-often-should-you-use-long-serves/).

**Drill prescription** — Lodziak's *"3rd Ball Drill 2"*: serve half-long backspin to the feeder's elbow, attack the weak return; *"Receive Drill 2"*: feeder serves long sidespin to backhand or elbow [[31]](https://www.tabletenniscoach.me.uk/10-training-drill-ideas-for-the-first-five-shots-in-a-rally/).

The canonical body cue across coaches: **lift the elbow, tip-up grip, "throw the tomahawk"** — brush starting at the upper-right rear of the ball, finish underneath [[42]](https://www.experttabletennis.com/tomahawk-serve/). Variations universally taught: sidespin, sidespin-topspin (brush up), sidespin-backspin (brush down) [[36]](https://www.pingskills.com/blog/tomahawk-serve-preview) [[38]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dwbEaAWuQUU).

**Placement:** Samson Dubina's tactical rule — serve from the forehand side into the receiver's wide forehand for maximum break-away angle [[41]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tqLxc6mxj6I); ExpertTableTennis says short to opponent's forehand 95% of the time [[42]](https://www.experttabletennis.com/tomahawk-serve/). Larry Hodges confirms the chaos mechanism — *"tremendous sidespin that breaks away from the receiver, frequently causing a last-second lunge … leading to many mistakes"* [[43]](https://www.tabletenniscoaching.com/node/144).

## Upgrade — reverse pendulum

Once the pendulum and tomahawk feel automatic, the reverse pendulum is the single highest-payoff addition.

| 3 | [Pendulum & Reverse Pendulum Serve](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cREC-uXp-KQ) [[47]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cREC-uXp-KQ) | PingSkills | ~797K views, Apr 2008 | Side-by-side visual contrast — useful when upgrading from pendulum |
| 4 | [Master the Reverse Pendulum Serve](https://www.tabletenniscoach.me.uk/video/master-the-reverse-pendulum-serve/) [[48]](https://www.tabletenniscoach.me.uk/video/master-the-reverse-pendulum-serve/) | Tom Lodziak × Tyla Anderson | full video lesson | Includes a return-challenge segment with Tom — see how it actually plays |
| 5 | [Learning KILLER Reverse Pendulum Serve](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T6hdfysn35E) [[45]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=T6hdfysn35E) | MLFM Table Tennis | 8:30, ~315K views, Nov 2019 | Popular alternative walkthrough for amateurs |

The 5-step pattern (TTD Academy, learn it in this order): **(1)** wrist-only feel, square to the table; **(2)** add forearm side-on, elbow lifted to create backswing space; **(3)** layer waist/shoulder rotation; **(4)** vary contact point — cut under for backspin, brush around the side for topspin; **(5)** drill solo (serves don't need a partner) [[44]](https://tabletennisdailyacademy.com/5-steps-to-master-the-reverse-pendulum-serve/).

EmRatThich frames the mechanics: racket moves outside-inward, generating right-to-left sidespin (opposite the regular pendulum); spin direction changes with racket angle and contact point; *"heavily used by professional players like Zhang Jike"* [[51]](https://pingsunday.com/reverse-pendulum-serve/).

## Suggested watch order (one week)

1. **Day 1.** Watch *4 killer serves* once [[26]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EuXKHxRcRbQ). Drill the short pendulum backspin off-table (Hodges progression) [[9]](https://blog.paddlepalace.com/2013/08/a-step-by-step-sequence-to-learning-pendulum-serves/) before going on-table.
2. **Day 2.** Watch *Backspin and No Spin Variations* [[13]](https://www.pingskills.com/tutorials/table-tennis-serving-strategy/back-spin-and-no-spin-serve-variations). Drill the pair from the same arm path; cue is contact-near-handle for no-spin [[14]](https://www.pingskills.com/table-tennis-forum/tips-for-a-basic-no-spin-serve).
3. **Day 3.** Watch *SUPER FAST long serve* with Drinkhall [[24]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n5JRm7DOjdw). 50 reps to the elbow, low toss, first bounce on your own endline.
4. **Day 4.** Watch *EASIEST Serve to Learn — Tomahawk* [[39]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_qOnY2Qep9c). Drill short tomahawks to opponent's forehand.
5. **Day 5.** Service-game audit — Lodziak's 3rd-ball drills [[31]](https://www.tabletenniscoach.me.uk/10-training-drill-ideas-for-the-first-five-shots-in-a-rally/).
6. **Day 6+.** Add the reverse pendulum via the TTD Academy 5 steps [[7]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=a2qDUlEe1ck) only after the first three serves are automatic.
