## The 14-day window

Across all six topic areas, the 2026 CFP cycle in Belgium, France, the Netherlands, and Germany is in its **late-tail phase**: most flagship events booked their speakers between January and mid-April. What's left is a tight cluster of deadlines bunched into the next two weeks, then a thinner second wave running into late June.

**Today, 30 April, two CFPs close at 23:59 CEST**: [BruCON 0x12](https://www.brucon.org/) in Mechelen [[1]](https://www.brucon.org/call-for-papers) and the German-language online [Power Summit](https://events.m365-summits.de/PowerSummit1415Juli2026/) [[2]](https://sessionize.com/power-summit-14-15-juli-2026/). BruCON is the only one of the two with material speaker support — €1000 travel plus lodging [[3]](https://sessionize.com/brucon-0x12/), exceptional for a community-run security con.

**Days 4–10**: [NeurIPS 2026](https://neurips.cc/) abstracts May 4 / paper May 6 — and crucially, the conference has a **Paris venue node** alongside Atlanta and Sydney [[4]](https://neurips.cc/Conferences/2026/Dates), making it one of the few in-region open AI venues. [OWASP AppSec Days France](https://www.owaspappsecdays.fr/) closes May 7 (FR or EN, 20-min lightning slot is the path of least resistance) [[5]](https://sessionize.com/owasp-appsec-days-france-2026); [escar Europe](https://escar.info/escar-europe) Bonn closes May 10 (automotive cyber only) [[6]](https://escar.info/escar-europe/call-for-papers); [code.talks Hamburg](https://codetalks.com/) closes May 15 [[7]](https://cfp.watch/).

## Travel-friendly picks if you want a real stage

For practitioners who want a paid trip and a sizable audience, the two cleanest submissions are **[Techorama NL](https://www.techorama.nl/)** (Utrecht, Oct 26–28; closes May 29; covers travel + hotel) [[8]](https://sessionize.com/techorama-2026-netherlands/) and **[DDC Cologne](https://www.developer-world.com/ddc)** (Nov 23–26; closes Jun 15; travel reimbursement + accommodation, no honorarium) [[9]](https://sessionize.com/dotnet-developer-conference-26/). Techorama NL also overlaps the architecture topic — its "Architecture & Leadership" track means a single submission can target either the .NET or architecture audience.

The architecture flagship in DACH is **[iSAQB Software Architecture Gathering](https://www.software-architecture-gathering.com/)** (Berlin, Nov 16–19) — its CFP **opens 5 May, closes 9 Jun** [[10]](https://www.software-architecture-gathering.com/call-for-papers/). Worth a calendar reminder.

## Where the calendar is empty

**France** is the thinnest market in this snapshot. [Devoxx France](https://www.devoxx.fr/en/), [dotJS](https://www.dotjs.io/), [React Paris](https://cfp.watch/cfps/2026-react-paris/), [Vue.js Amsterdam](https://vuejs.amsterdam/), [leHACK](https://lehack.org/), and [Pass the SALT](https://2026.pass-the-salt.org/) have all either happened or closed their CFPs [[11]](https://www.devoxx.fr/en/) [[12]](https://www.dotjs.io/) [[13]](https://lehack.org/). What's left: [OWASP AppSec Days](https://www.owaspappsecdays.fr/2026/) (May 7), [MWCP Paris](https://modern-workplace.pro/)'s rolling Microsoft-community submissions [[14]](https://modern-workplace.pro/), [DevFest Paris](https://devfest.gdgparis.fr/) (Jun 30), and the [NeurIPS](https://neurips.cc/) Paris node. **NewCrafts Paris is on hiatus** for 2026 and returns in 2027.

**Pure-React submitters** have exactly one option: [React Day Berlin](https://reactday.berlin/) on Aug 12 — and it's explicitly unpaid (`speaking fee No`) per cfp.watch [[7]](https://cfp.watch/). The big Amsterdam React events ([JSNation](https://www.papercall.io/jsnation), [React Summit](https://gitnation.com/events/react-summit-2026)) closed their windows in March.

## Two near-future openings worth the wait

[Devoxx Belgium](https://devoxx.be/) opens its CFP **1 June** (closes Jul 17; event Oct 5–9 in Antwerp) [[15]](https://devoxx.be/), and [iSAQB SAG](https://www.software-architecture-gathering.com/) opens 5 May [[10]](https://www.software-architecture-gathering.com/call-for-papers/). Both are higher-signal than anything closing this week. If a single, well-prepared talk can be submitted multiple places, the optimal sequence is: file by today ([BruCON](https://www.brucon.org/)), submit during the 4–15 May wave, then resubmit refined versions to [Devoxx BE](https://devoxx.be/) and [iSAQB SAG](https://www.software-architecture-gathering.com/) in early June.

The sharpest open question this snapshot leaves: **why is the BE/NL/DE 2026 cycle now this front-loaded?** Five years ago a 30 April look would have surfaced ten flagship CFPs still open; today it surfaces three (security) and a handful of mid-tier practitioner events. Worth tracking next year whether Sessionize/papercall.io's earlier-opening defaults are pulling deadlines forward across the board.
