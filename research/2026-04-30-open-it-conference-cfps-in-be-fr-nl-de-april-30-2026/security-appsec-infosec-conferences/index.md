## Notes on the open three

[**BruCON**](https://www.brucon.org/) rewards in-person attendance: speakers get up to €1000 reimbursed and lodging covered, which is unusual for a community-led con and worth the rush submission [[2]](https://sessionize.com/brucon-0x12/). They take both 45-min talks and full workshops, plus 2/3-day trainings — different review tracks, same deadline [[1]](https://www.brucon.org/call-for-papers).

[**OWASP AppSec Days France**](https://www.owaspappsecdays.fr/) is brand-new in Paris (the first local OWASP day there) and explicitly accepts French *or* English [[4]](https://www.owaspappsecdays.fr/2026/). Commercial pitches are rejected, but vendor talks built around practical case studies are fine [[3]](https://sessionize.com/owasp-appsec-days-france-2026). Lightning slot (20m) is the path of least resistance if you're submitting cold.

[**escar Europe**](https://escar.info/escar-europe) is the narrowest of the three — submit only if your work is automotive (in-vehicle networks, V2X, UN R155 / ISO 21434 compliance, EV charging security, secure ML in vehicles) [[7]](https://escar.info/escar-europe). Two parallel tracks (Scientific + Industry) share the May 10 deadline; pick the one that matches your evidence base [[6]](https://easychair.org/cfp/24th-escarEurope2026).
