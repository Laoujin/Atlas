## How to read this list

Curated for **signal-to-noise**, not reach. Most "top AI Twitter" articles rank on follower counts; that surfaces Sam Altman (4.2M) and Lex Fridman (4.2M) [[16]](https://x.feedspot.com/artificial_intelligence_twitter_influencers/) — fine for headlines, weak for technical depth. The picks below are the ones repeatedly named by practitioners (KDnuggets [[1]](https://www.kdnuggets.com/10-best-x-twitter-accounts-to-follow-for-llm-updates), tkainrad's "people who actually tweet about software engineering" [[19]](https://tkainrad.dev/posts/twitter-accounts-in-software-engineering/), WeAreDevelopers [[20]](https://www.wearedevelopers.com/en/magazine/294/best-x-twitter-accounts-for-developers), HyperWrite [[29]](https://www.hyperwriteai.com/blog/top-ai-influencers-to-follow-on-twitter)) plus follow-graph evidence (e.g. Karpathy's public endorsement of Willison [[17]](https://x.com/karpathy/status/1933582359347278246)).

## What to skip

- **"Top 100 AI influencers" feedspot-style rankings** [[16]](https://x.feedspot.com/artificial_intelligence_twitter_influencers/) — sorted by reach, not signal. Useful only as a starting roster.
- **Mega-accounts whose content has shifted to commentary/politics** — even technically-credentialed accounts (LeCun, others) have drifted; mute, don't unfollow, if you still want occasional company-strategy signal.
- **AI-news aggregator accounts** as your primary feed — they re-tweet what AK [[6]](https://x.com/_akhaliq) and DAIR.AI [[5]](https://x.com/dair_ai) already surfaced first, with more noise.

## Filter discipline

Two operational habits that matter more than which accounts you add:

1. **Use Lists, not the home feed.** Build a private "AI signal" List with the top section above; pin its column.
2. **Mute aggressively.** Mute words like *"thread"*, *"🧵"*, *"BREAKING"*, *"this changes everything"* — the noise accounts use them; the signal accounts don't.

## Caveats

- Follower counts above [[16]](https://x.feedspot.com/artificial_intelligence_twitter_influencers/) are point-in-time (early 2026) and drift quickly.
- X profiles can go quiet — Karpathy went quiet for stretches in 2024 [[2]](https://x.com/karpathy); Simon Willison's blog is more reliable than his X for the depth-first reader [[17]](https://x.com/karpathy/status/1933582359347278246).
- For long-form depth, X is a discovery layer — pair this list with the [bloggers/newsletter](../long-form-bloggers-newsletter-authors/) and [YouTube](../youtube-channels/) shortlists from the same parent research.
