## What changed since 2024

Three shifts shape this list. First, the AI-news YouTube category is now packaging-driven — channels like [Wes Roth](https://www.youtube.com/@WesRoth) openly admit their "outrageous" thumbnails are clickbait that "work", which makes the "hype filter" tier ([AI Explained](https://www.youtube.com/@aiexplained-official), [AI Daily Brief](https://www.youtube.com/@AIDailyBrief)) disproportionately valuable [[17]](https://publicservicesalliance.org/2025/05/29/wes-roth-on-singularity-and-asi-speculation/)[[14]](https://www.atakinteractive.com/blog/17-ai-youtubers-were-actually-watching-right-now). Second, an entire new tier emerged in 2024-2026: agent-builders shipping [Cursor](https://cursor.com/) / [Claude Code](https://www.anthropic.com/claude-code) / [n8n](https://n8n.io/) pipelines on camera with companion repos [[40]](https://agenticengineer.com/tactical-agentic-coding)[[41]](https://github.com/disler/claude-code-hooks-multi-agent-observability) ⭐ 1.4k. Third, the long-form interview format consolidated around [Dwarkesh](https://www.dwarkesh.com/), [Latent Space](https://www.latent.space/) and [MLST](https://www.mlst.ai/) as the technically-deep tier. [Lex](https://lexfridman.com/) remains the public-discourse heavyweight [[64]](https://arize.com/ai-podcasts/)[[52]](https://www.tldl.io/resources/best-ai-podcasts-2026).

## AI research & paper explainers

The technical-depth tier has stayed small. [Yannic Kilcher](https://www.youtube.com/c/YannicKilcher) (~280K subs) does 45+ minute paragraph-by-paragraph paper walkthroughs and pushes back when methods don't justify hype [[1]](https://www.youtube.com/c/YannicKilcher)[[2]](https://aiitinfluencers.com/articles/yannic-kilcher). [3Blue1Brown](https://www.3blue1brown.com/) (2.2M subs, 50M+ views on the deep-learning series) is the canonical visuals-first foundation course [[3]](https://www.3blue1brown.com/lessons/neural-networks/). [Andrej Karpathy](https://karpathy.ai/)'s *Neural Networks: Zero to Hero* builds NNs from backprop through GPTs in code and is the single most-recommended free DL course [[9]](https://karpathy.ai/zero-to-hero.html)[[65]](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLXYLzZ3XzIbi4lL43O6fIU_ojuZwBO6vi); his 4-hour "Let's reproduce GPT-2 (124M)" [[67]](https://x.com/karpathy/status/1799949853289804266) and 3h31m "Deep Dive into LLMs like ChatGPT" [[66]](https://x.com/karpathy/status/1887211193099825254) are the 2024-2025 supplements.

The implementation-from-scratch sub-tier — frequently grouped on AI-researcher Twitter — is [Umar Jamil](https://www.youtube.com/@umarjamilai) (Transformer / Diffusion / LLM in PyTorch with public slides + GitHub code) [[8]](https://www.youtube.com/@umarjamilai), [Tunadorable](https://www.youtube.com/@Tunadorable) (paper review, implementation, Triton kernels) [[7]](https://www.youtube.com/@Tunadorable), and [bycloud](https://bycloud.ai/) (~185K subs, technical LLM-research summaries plus the *AI Timeline* newsletter and findmypapers.ai paper-search engine) [[6]](https://www.youtube.com/@bycloudAI)[[21]](https://bycloud.ai/).

## AI news & discourse — signal vs. hype

The AI-news tier stratifies cleanly along a signal-vs-packaging axis. [**AI Explained**](https://www.youtube.com/@aiexplained-official) (Philip, ~400K subs) is the consensus "hype filter": meticulous research-backed analysis whose newsletter (literally titled *Signal to Noise*) is reportedly read by staff at OpenAI, Microsoft and DeepMind [[14]](https://www.atakinteractive.com/blog/17-ai-youtubers-were-actually-watching-right-now)[[15]](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCNJ1Ymd5yFuUPtn21xtRbbw)[[13]](https://www.youtube.com/@aiexplained-official). [**The AI Daily Brief**](https://www.youtube.com/@AIDailyBrief) with Nathaniel Whittemore (~134K YouTube subs, well-rated on Apple Podcasts) ships 10–25-minute daily episodes that "prioritize depth over sensationalism, consistency over viral moments" — the other clear analyst pick [[19]](https://www.eartisan.co.uk/the-voice-thats-shaping-ai-discourse-how-the-ai-daily-brief-became-essential-listening-in-the-age-of-artificial-intelligence-2/)[[20]](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/the-ai-daily-brief-artificial-intelligence-news/id1680633614).

[**Matt Wolfe**](https://www.youtube.com/@mreflow) (~925K subs, [FutureTools.io](https://www.futuretools.io/), ~250K newsletter) is the best generalist-aggregator if you only subscribe to one — telling viewers "which three [tools] actually matter" out of 50 weekly launches [[16]](https://www.youtube.com/@mreflow)[[51]](https://mattwolfe.com/). [**Matthew Berman**](https://www.youtube.com/@matthew_berman) (~480K subs, ~4 vids/wk, 57M views) covers AI news, model comparisons, and generative-art topics — useful for tracking releases, but lighter on actually-built agents than the [IndyDevDan](https://www.youtube.com/@indydevdan) / [Cole Medin](https://www.youtube.com/@ColeMedin) tier [[49]](https://app.thoughtleaders.io/youtube/matthew-berman). [**Wes Roth**](https://www.youtube.com/@WesRoth) (~293K subs) is the canonical split-reputation case: he openly admits his thumbnails are "outrageous" clickbait that "work", while his on-camera discourse is "measured and chock full of facts" — useful, but the packaging triggers hype-skepticism [[17]](https://publicservicesalliance.org/2025/05/29/wes-roth-on-singularity-and-asi-speculation/)[[18]](https://www.youtube.com/@WesRoth). [**MattVidPro**](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC5Wz4fFacYuON6IKbhSa7Zw) and [**AI Search**](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCIgnGlGkVRhd4qNFcEwLL4A) sit in the broader generalist/tool-review tier — recommended in beginner-accessible roundups but absent from analyst-tier shortlists [[22]](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UC5Wz4fFacYuON6IKbhSa7Zw)[[23]](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCIgnGlGkVRhd4qNFcEwLL4A).

## Software engineering & programming

The 2026 community consensus splits cleanly by purpose. **Fireship** (Jeff Delaney, ~4.1M subs, ~0.75% monthly growth) is the unanimous default for staying current via 100-second explainers and the weekly Code Report [[24]](https://www.youtube.com/@Fireship)[[25]](https://marcgg.com/blog/2025/02/12/dev-youtube/). **ThePrimeagen** (~484K, ex-Netflix, two channels — Vim/Neovim, Rust, TypeScript, performance) and **Theo Browne / t3.gg** (~492K by late 2025, opinionated TypeScript/React/full-stack) are the most-cited working-engineer commentary picks [[26]](https://www.youtube.com/@ThePrimeagen)[[27]](https://www.youtube.com/@t3dotgg).

For backend depth, **Hussein Nasser** (~445K) is the consensus pick — databases, proxies, networking protocols, kernel-level optimizations [[28]](https://www.youtube.com/@hnasr). **Dave Farley's Continuous Delivery** is the senior pick for architecture and team practice, often featuring Kent Beck, Sam Newman, Kevlin Henney and Daniel Terhorst-North rather than tooling tutorials [[29]](https://www.youtube.com/@ContinuousDelivery). **ArjanCodes** is the recommended Python channel for design patterns and code architecture, not syntax [[30]](https://www.arjancodes.com/). **Web Dev Simplified** (1.7M+, Kyle Cook) and **Ben Awad** (~300K, React/GraphQL/TypeScript, including the famous 14-hour fullstack tutorial) hold the front-end tutorial slots [[31]](https://www.youtube.com/@WebDevSimplified)[[32]](https://www.youtube.com/@bawad). **Coderized** publishes rare but highly-polished principled-coding essays [[33]](https://www.youtube.com/@coderized). **Coding Train** (Daniel Shiffman, NYU ITP) remains canonical for creative coding [[34]](https://thecodingtrain.com/).

For system design specifically, **ByteByteGo** (1.37M, Alex Xu), **Hello Interview** ('goat prep' on Reddit/Blind), **Jordan Has No Life** (staff-level distributed systems) and **Arpit Bhayani** round out the 2026 picks alongside Hussein Nasser [[36]](https://learnwithpath.com/blog/best-youtube-channels-for-system-design-2026). The web-dev top-10 also includes Kevin Powell, Jack Herrington, Net Ninja, Traversy Media and ByteGrad [[37]](https://learnwithpath.com/blog/best-youtube-channels-for-web-development-2026). For senior-IC career content, **A Life Engineered** (Steve Huynh, ex-Amazon Principal) is the most-cited [[38]](https://www.youtube.com/@ALifeEngineered). HN's advanced-programming thread additionally champions Jon Gjengset (Rust), Jacob Sorber (C/systems), and the ACM SIGPLAN talks archive [[35]](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=24671019).

## Build-with-AI / agentic-dev wave

The 2024-2026 wave splits by what the creator does on camera. **IndyDevDan** ships hands-on Claude Code agentic-engineering content — sub-agents, hooks, multi-agent observability, custom Claude Code SDK agents — explicitly framed as "anti-hype" tactical engineering [[39]](https://www.youtube.com/@indydevdan/videos)[[40]](https://agenticengineer.com/tactical-agentic-coding); the companion repo `disler/claude-code-hooks-multi-agent-observability` [[41]](https://github.com/disler/claude-code-hooks-multi-agent-observability) ⭐ 1.4k confirms the demos are runnable artefacts rather than slideware. **Cole Medin** covers AI agents, RAG, local LLMs, pragmatically mixing code with no-code (LangChain, LangGraph, n8n, Ollama, Supabase) and has actively pushed past surface "vibe coding" into context engineering for production agents [[42]](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/community/learn-with/cole-medin/)[[43]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7zZtxUW6dhc).

**Sam Witteveen** is the framework-walkthrough specialist — CrewAI, SmolAgents, Pydantic AI, LangGraph, Google's A2A protocol, Microsoft's Magentic-One — backed by 11 years in DL and agent work since early 2023 [[44]](https://www.youtube.com/@samwitteveenai/videos)[[45]](https://github.com/samwit). **AI Jason** (Jason Zhou) is the practical builder-channel for AI agents, LangChain, Auto-GPT and multi-agent frameworks; he runs a 1,200+ member paid AI Builder Club community (co-built with Inflect Labs) covering Cursor, MCP and agent courses, evidence of educator standing rather than just creator [[89]](https://www.ai-jason.com/)[[90]](https://www.aibuilderclub.com/)[[91]](https://www.skool.com/ai-builder-club/about). **Mervin Praison** is high-signal *for his own framework* `MervinPraison/PraisonAI` [[46]](https://github.com/MervinPraison/PraisonAI) ⭐ 7.0k but self-referential. **Riley Brown** owns the vibe-coding-with-Cursor-voice niche aimed at non-coders [[47]](https://x.com/rileybrown_ai/status/1887524152380158251). **David Ondrej** (Planet AI, ~321K subs) leans monetisation-pitch over engineering depth [[48]](https://app.thoughtleaders.io/youtube/david-ondrej). For n8n/Make automation specifically, **Nate Herk** and **Nick Saraev** are the recommended builders; **Greg Isenberg** is AI-startup-ideas, not on-camera engineering [[50]](https://medium.com/ai-engineers/youtube-channels-for-ai-engineers-d9b1d96a07d7). ## Long-form interview podcasts on YouTube

**Dwarkesh Patel** runs the breakout technical-interview show — biweekly, 60–120-minute interviews with "exhaustive preparation, technical depth, and willingness to challenge premises", earning him the description of "the primary oral historian of the artificial intelligence revolution" [[53]](https://eutechfuture.com/tech-thought-leaders/dwarkesh-patel-leading-tech-interviewer-shaping-ai-journalism/); 2025-26 episodes feature Karpathy, Sutskever, Nadella, Zuckerberg, Hassabis and Amodei, and he is widely described as having "rose from nowhere to become Silicon Valley's favourite podcaster" [[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dwarkesh_Patel). **Latent Space** (swyx & Alessio) is the AI-engineer canonical reference: a top-10 US Tech show that hit 10M+ readers/listeners in 2025, whose episodes "become reference material passed between engineering teams" [[55]](https://www.latent.space/about). **Machine Learning Street Talk** (Tim Scarfe, Keith Duggar) is positioned as "the highest-rated technical AI podcast on Spotify" with the deepest research/cognitive-science framing — explicitly "not for the faint of heart" [[11]](https://www.youtube.com/c/MachineLearningStreetTalk)[[56]](https://www.mlst.ai/about).

The broader-audience tier: **Lex Fridman** remains the heavyweight for 3–4-hour researcher conversations; his 2025 Amodei and Hassabis episodes "shaped public discourse" [[52]](https://www.tldl.io/resources/best-ai-podcasts-2026). ⚠ Counter-reading is well-corroborated and not fringe: Helen Lewis writes Fridman "does not maintain even a thin veneer of journalistic detachment", *The Verge*'s Elizabeth Lopatto calls him a "softball interviewer" [[84]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lex_Fridman); HN threads since 2020 flag the same pattern — "Lex seems to never do any research on the guest... it's all un-interesting, softball questions that don't challenge" [[85]](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=32347619)[[86]](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=21968948); a long-form critique notes Fridman "didn't follow up on technical details about model improvements and would rather talk about whether the model can feel love" in the Altman interview [[87]](https://winningwp.com/the-problem-with-lex-fridman-interviews-and-why-an-ai-interviewer-could-be-infinitely-better/); industry commentary observes that powerful guests treat the show as "a friendlier alternative to journalism" with "narrative control without accountability" [[88]](https://thedeepdive.ca/why-lex-fridman-hate/). Treat as primary-source archive, not analysis. **No Priors** (Sarah Guo & Elad Gil) is the founder/investor lens, weekly, with grounded forecasts on agents and continual learning [[59]](https://www.youtube.com/@NoPriorsPodcast). **Cognitive Revolution** is biweekly with builder/live-player analysis — correctly hosted by Nathan Labenz (not "Nathaniel Whittemore") and Erik Torenberg [[57]](https://www.cognitiverevolution.ai/).

**BG2** (Brad Gerstner & Bill Gurley) lands CEO-tier guests like Jensen Huang, Satya Nadella and OpenAI's Nick Turley for markets/capital framing [[58]](https://www.bg2pod.com/). **a16z's AI + a16z** plus the annual *Big Ideas* series cover infrastructure and tooling from a deployed-capital perspective [[61]](https://a16z.com/podcasts/ai-a16z/). **20VC** is more VC-news than AI-substance and draws mixed reviews [[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Stebbings). **Acquired** remains best-in-class for tech-history (1M+ listeners/episode, monthly multi-hour case studies) [[60]](https://www.acquired.fm/about). **Patrick Collison** is best treated as a high-signal guest (Dwarkesh, a16z, Cursor) rather than a host of his own regular channel [[63]](https://www.dwarkesh.com/p/patrick-collison). 2026 industry roundups consistently cluster Dwarkesh, Latent Space, MLST, No Priors and Cognitive Revolution as the top tier for engineers and founders [[64]](https://arize.com/ai-podcasts/).

## Labs, lecturers & conferences

The lecturer tier centres on **Andrej Karpathy**: *Neural Networks: Zero to Hero* is an 8-lecture progression from micrograd backprop through MLPs, WaveNet, GPT and a custom tokenizer [[9]](https://karpathy.ai/zero-to-hero.html)[[65]](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLXYLzZ3XzIbi4lL43O6fIU_ojuZwBO6vi), extended in mid-2024 by the 4-hour "Let's reproduce GPT-2 (124M)" build-from-scratch lecture [[67]](https://x.com/karpathy/status/1799949853289804266) and in early 2025 by the 3h31m "Deep Dive into LLMs like ChatGPT" general-audience overview [[66]](https://x.com/karpathy/status/1887211193099825254). **Sebastian Raschka** complements this with a "Build a Large Language Model (From Scratch)" code-along plus 2026 commentary on RLVR, GRPO and reasoning LLMs [[79]](https://www.youtube.com/c/SebastianRaschka). **Jeremy Howard / fast.ai** still ships the free 8-lesson *Practical Deep Learning for Coders* on YouTube [[80]](https://course.fast.ai/).

The university tier delivers reliable yearly drops: **Stanford CS25 Transformers United** is on its sixth iteration in 2026 with rotating frontier speakers (Hinton, Vaswani, Karpathy) and a new V6 lecture posted in April 2026 [[68]](https://web.stanford.edu/class/cs25/)[[69]](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLoROMvodv4rNiJRchCzutFw5ItR_Z27CM)[[70]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GBd7iuJkW08). **MIT 6.S191** ships a fully refreshed playlist every January IAP, with the 2026 edition graded for credit [[71]](https://introtodeeplearning.com/)[[72]](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLtBw6njQRU-rwp5__7C0oIVt26ZgjG9NI). **CS224N** (Manning, NLP) and **CS231N** (computer vision, Spring 2025 lectures live) remain canonical text/CV references [[73]](https://web.stanford.edu/class/cs224n/)[[74]](https://cs231n.stanford.edu/). **DeepLearning.AI** is the practitioner anchor, releasing free 1–2 hour short courses co-built with OpenAI, Anthropic, LangChain, Google and AWS [[75]](https://www.youtube.com/c/deeplearningai).

The lab/conference tier covers **Google DeepMind**'s channel (Hannah Fry's podcast returning in 2026, plus keynotes) [[76]](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCP7jMXSY2xbc3KCAE0MHQ-A), **Simons Institute** (full workshop talks including the 2026 Federated Learning program) [[77]](https://www.youtube.com/user/SimonsInstitute), **Microsoft Research**'s Research Forum series [[78]](https://www.youtube.com/user/MicrosoftResearch) and **Hugging Face**'s course/community playlist [[81]](https://www.youtube.com/HuggingFace).

The frontier-lab official channels behave very differently and only one is a reasonable subscription. **Anthropic** (`@anthropic-ai`, 569K subs / 168 videos as of April 2026) posts roughly weekly — short Opus 4.6 / Cowork / Claude Code / MCP demos plus longer interpretability and safety pieces like "What is AI reward hacking?" and "When AIs act emotional"; useful as a secondary follow but dominated by launch announcements [[92]](https://www.youtube.com/@anthropic-ai/videos)[[98]](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-design-anthropic-labs). **OpenAI** (`@OpenAI`, 1.94M subs / 525 videos) is overwhelmingly product-marketing — ChatGPT use-case ads, Codex spotlights, GPT-5.x demo clips, the OpenAI Podcast and replays of `openai.com/live` launch streams — high cadence, low signal-to-press-release ratio; treat as the official launch-replay archive, not a learning channel [[93]](https://www.youtube.com/@OpenAI/videos)[[96]](https://openai.com/devday/). **NeurIPS** has no official YouTube channel for paper talks — orals, tutorials and invited talks live on `nips.cc/virtual/<year>` about a month after the event ("We do not use Whova for videos"), and even Sutskever's 2024 Test-of-Time keynote surfaces on YouTube only as third-party reuploads [[94]](https://nips.cc/virtual/2024/index.html)[[95]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1yvBqasHLZs)[[97]](https://blog.neurips.cc/2024/11/27/announcing-the-neurips-2024-test-of-time-paper-awards/). Curated 2026 "best AI YouTube" lists targeting practitioners reflect this pattern — they lead with educator/explainer channels (Two Minute Papers, DeepLearning.AI, AI Explained, Yannic Kilcher, Dwarkesh) and do not single out OpenAI, Anthropic or NeurIPS [[99]](https://medium.com/the-ai-studio/these-10-ai-youtube-channels-are-better-than-any-2-000-course-0b54a0d585a1).

## What to skip or actively de-noise

- **Two Minute Papers.** Once the canonical low-effort summarizer; HN regulars in 2025 said the "fake-excitement" pattern now overshadows actual algorithm explanation [[4]](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=45762338).
- **"GPT-X JUST CHANGED EVERYTHING" reaction channels.** The community frame is that "thousands of AI channels are recycling the same news with the same clickbait" [[17]](https://publicservicesalliance.org/2025/05/29/wes-roth-on-singularity-and-asi-speculation/) — AI Explained, AI Daily Brief, bycloud and Matthew Berman cover the same news with substantially more substance.
- **"Make money with AI" framing.** David Ondrej and the broader monetisation tier optimise the funnel, not the engineering [[48]](https://app.thoughtleaders.io/youtube/david-ondrej). Useful as market signal, low signal as practitioner content.
- **20VC for AI-substance.** Strong VC reporting; mixed for technical AI [[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Stebbings).

## Coverage gaps

- Direct numerical YouTube subscriber counts are uneven across this list — the analyst/aggregator tier is well-instrumented (Fireship, ThePrimeagen, Theo, Matt Wolfe, Berman); some lecturer / smaller lab channels report differently or update slowly.
- Direct subreddit-thread quotes for the AI Explained / AI Daily Brief analyst-tier rating could not be retrieved in this run (reddit.com search and WebFetch were unreliable); the consensus is corroborated through synthesis and aggregator pieces but a future pass should harden it with raw r/MachineLearning, r/singularity and r/OpenAI threads.
- The "skip" verdict on Two Minute Papers rests on a single substantive HN thread; a second venue criticising the channel's drift was not surfaced here.
- Non-English channels (Chinese-language ML, European-language SWE, etc.) are out of scope and worth a dedicated pass.
